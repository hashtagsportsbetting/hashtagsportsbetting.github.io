🏆 Fun fact · it has been done
In 2008 a Yorkshireman named Fred Craggs put 50p on eight horses, watched every one win, and strolled out of William Hill a millionaire — the payout capped at £1m, the sheer cheek of it. Mick Gibbs went further still: 30p, fifteen football matches, odds north of 1.6 million to one, half a million quid collected the night Bayern saw off Valencia. So sixteen legs for a euro-fifty isn’t madness. It’s heritage.
No tips, no promises. One euro-fifty, all in, eyes open, brain off. First balls at noon — after that it’s out of my hands and into the laps of sixteen strangers.
