Third parlay of the week. Third to die. The pattern isn’t subtle, and I won’t pretend otherwise: four of these six legs landed, two didn’t, and the maths of an accumulator means two is plenty.

The four that did their job

Boitan came from a set down to beat Turcanu 3-6 6-4 6-4 in Constanta. Pegula handled the Keys handicap, surviving two tie-breaks to win 7-6 7-6 — a near thing, but a thing. Yilmaz saw off Senthil Kumar in Kayseri. And Fenerbahçe edged Beşiktaş 77-75 in the Turkish league. Four legs, no complaints. Then the two that turned a promising slip into a museum piece.

🔁
The first betrayal — De Minaur, again
Two days ago, De Minaur won me a leg at Queen’s against Shapovalov. I went back to the same well. This time he drew Brandon Nakashima in the quarter-finals — world No.32, unbeaten on grass this month, and aggressive from the first ball.
↳ATP Tour · Nakashima upsets De Minaur 7-5 6-3 ✅

I backed the same player twice in three days. He paid once and bit me once. That’s not a system. That’s a relationship.

— C.C. Farah
The grass favourite who’d run out of road

De Minaur is one of the best grass-courters on tour — world No.6, top seed, fresh off a grass final five days earlier (a narrow loss to Majchrzak). That last fact was the problem hiding in plain sight: a man who’d just played a final, then a quick turnaround to London, against an opponent in form. Nakashima struck cleanly and won 7-5 6-3 — what the ATP called “uncharacteristic errors” from De Minaur, and the American’s third career top-10 win. The price said De Minaur at 1.387. The grass said otherwise.

🏀
The second betrayal — Bayern, with the title in hand
Game 4 of the German BBL final. Bayern led the series 2-1, chasing a historic third straight championship, with two chances to close it out. My leg needed them to win one. Alba Berlin, at home and refusing to go home, beat them 71-61 to force a deciding Game 5.
↳Eurohoops · BBL Finals series ✅

Backing the favourite to wrap up a final is the most reasonable bet in the world — right up until the underdog decides it isn’t ready to go home.

— C.C. Farah

−€15.00. Two favourites, two refusals. It can only get better — I’ll be patient. Do I suck at parlays? Probably. Wouldn’t be the first, would I? Too early to say. Wait and see. 🙈
