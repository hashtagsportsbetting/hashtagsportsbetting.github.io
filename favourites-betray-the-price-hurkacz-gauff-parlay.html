died on the sixth floor.

Seven legs. Five winners. Two favourites that did not read the script — Hurkacz, beaten at home by Altmaier, and Gauff, who led Badosa 6-1 and then folded. €5 staked, €0 back. The point of #ZOOM IN is the autopsy, not the trophy cabinet.

I do not usually file the losers — but this one earns the scalpel, because five of these seven were exactly right. The market and I agreed on the easy ones. We disagreed on the only two that mattered.

The five that behaved

De Minaur swept Shapovalov 6-4 6-1 at Queen’s — his sixth straight win over the Canadian, with the extra motivation of matching fiancée Katie Boulter’s semi-final run the week before. Shelton beat Sonego, Svrcina handled Gombos, Brazil dismissed France 3-0 at 1.055, and Fenerbahçe saw off Beşiktaş. Five clean reads. The slip was one missed heartbeat from paying — and tennis supplied two.

📊
Signal — Hurkacz: the name, not the form
Hurkacz was the favourite at roughly 79% (-375), fresh off a 6-3 6-2 win over Rublev. But he had just dropped out of the top 100 for the first time since 2018. The price was paying his name, not his June. Altmaier, world No.81, was at home in Halle — and came from a set down to win 3-6 6-3 7-5 with 52 winners.
↳ATP Tour · Halle result (Altmaier 3-6 6-3 7-5) ✅

The board said Hurkacz. I said Hurkacz. We were both being lazy.

— C.C. Farah
The wildcard who’d forgotten how to lose
🩹
Entry context — Badosa / Gauff, Berlin
Gauff, No.5 seed, first grass match of the season, eighteen days off since Roland-Garros. Badosa, world No.142, a wildcard on a five-match losing streak. On paper, a formality. The first set was a formality — Gauff took it 6-1. Then the paper caught fire: Gauff’s first-serve percentage fell set by set, and Badosa won 1-6 6-3 6-2.
↳WTA · Badosa upsets Gauff ✅

The edge isn’t in knowing who’s better. It’s in knowing what the market hasn’t priced yet. On these two, the market and I made the same mistake — and when you both make the same mistake, you don’t get paid for being contrarian. You keep each other company on the way down.

— C.C. Farah

−€5.00. Five correct legs, two favourites that betrayed the price. Both upsets were available in the pre-match data — Hurkacz’s ranking slide, Gauff’s grass record, Altmaier’s home form, Badosa’s revival. I read both signals. I weighted neither.
