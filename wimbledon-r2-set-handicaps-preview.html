⏳ PRE-MATCH · AWAITING RESULT
The same convictions as the seven-fold next door, only downgraded from ambition to insurance. Instead of asking these players to win, we mostly ask them not to be swept: a set and a half of cover on the favourites, a deeper two and a half on the one five-setter, and a single man — Davidovich Fokina — we still trust to win outright. Fifteen quid, lower odds, higher floor.
The insurance policy

Anna Kalinskaya +1.5 sets at 1.195 is the template for the whole slip: against Diane Parry she now only has to avoid losing in straight sets. Given Parry’s 4-18 record against top-20 players and Kalinskaya’s 4-1 recent run through Grand Slam second rounds, a 0-2 collapse is the single outcome we are paying a small premium to rule out. The same logic covers Katerina Siniakova +1.5 (1.21) against Nikola Bartunkova, Mirra Andreeva +1.5 (1.19) against former champion Barbora Krejcikova, and Belinda Bencic +1.5 (1.12) against Xinyu Wang — three players we fancy to win outright on the other slip, insured here against the freak straight-sets afternoon.

Roman Safiullin +2.5 sets at 1.115 earns the deepest buffer of the lot, and for good reason. His match against Botic van de Zandschulp is the best-of-five, and Safiullin is gloriously unreliable — so rather than the set we asked of him elsewhere, here we simply need him not to be washed off court in a straight-sets whitewash. Against a van de Zandschulp who has reached the third round here once in five tries and arrives short of rhythm, that is about as safe as a grass-court leg gets.

The one we still back to win

Only Alejandro Davidovich Fokina (1.479) is asked for the full result, and he is asked for it on both slips because the conviction is genuine. Five straight wins through the grass swing, a semi-final scalp of this very opponent in Mallorca last week, and a Fabian Marozsan who has never reached a Wimbledon third round and sits 10-12 for his career on the surface. If any single leg on this ticket deserves to go uninsured, it is this one.

The stranger in Cary

Honesty, since it is the entire point of this website: the seventh leg is the one I know least about. Rio Noguchi +1.5 sets (1.22) comes not from Wimbledon but from the Cary Challenger on the American hard courts, bolted on as a low-variance insurance line rather than a considered read. A set and a half of cover on a Challenger favourite is a modest ask, and I will not dress it up as anything cleverer than that.

⚡ Fun fact · the man we refuse to insure
Every player on this slip gets a handicap cushion except one. We backed Alejandro Davidovich Fokina to beat Fabian Marozsan in the Mallorca semi-finals barely a week ago — so on the rematch, buying insurance on him felt like an insult to the read. He wins outright, or the slip was simply wrong about him.
A ceiling next door, a floor over here. Six players who merely must not be swept, one Challenger filler, and a single Spaniard we back without a safety net. Lower odds are the price of a higher probability — and the tennis, as ever, reserves the right to make fools of both slips at once.
