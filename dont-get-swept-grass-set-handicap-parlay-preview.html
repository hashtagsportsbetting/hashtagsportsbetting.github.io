Here's a quieter way to back a hunch. A +1.5 set handicap means my man can lose the match outright and I still collect, provided he takes one set on the way down. It's grass season, the lawn rewards the players built for it, and I've found five matches where the favourite — or the man on the right surface — only has to avoid a straight-sets collapse.
ATP Eastbourne · R16 · Grass
Gabriel Diallo v Tomas Martín Etcheverry
Diallo +1.5 sets
“Horrendous 5-13 all time on grass — can't be the favourite whatever the ranking says.”
— Tenngrand
Both men are off-form, but only one can play on grass, and it isn't the Argentine (5-13 there against 317 clay wins). Diallo has a real grass pedigree — 9-3 on it in 2025 — and the room agrees: Dimers 55%, bookmakers -140. I only need him competitive. The +1.5 does the rest.
ATP Eastbourne · R16 · Grass
Toby Samuel v Thiago Agustín Tirante
Samuel +1.5 sets
“A natural clay-courter — heavier ball, built for slower surfaces.”
— Last Word on Sports
For this leg to fail, a clay specialist (3-5 on grass) has to sweep a home grass-courter in straight sets, in front of his own crowd. Samuel is at home on the surface with the local support. I'll take that price.
For England Samuel — a set will do.

Harris +1.5 sets

For England Harris — hold a set and we're fine.

McDonald +1.5 sets

The shortest price here, and you can see why. Asking a clay terrier (7-17 on grass against 522-321 on clay) to beat a fast-court American by two clear sets on grass is asking a great deal. I'm not asking it. I just need McDonald upright.
Wimbledon Qualifying · Grass
Tristan Schoolkate v Daniel Evans
Schoolkate +1.5 sets
“My body hasn't been great, and I no longer want to put myself through the training.”
— Dan Evans, on retiring
The one I take no pleasure in, so let me be careful. Evans is a grass legend in the literal sense — a three-time champion on home grass and the first man in 23 years to win three Challenger titles on the surface. The talent never left. But the body has, and he's said so himself. Thirty-six, a chronic wrist problem, ranked 244th, one last Wimbledon to chase. I'm not betting against the player — I'm betting the body can no longer produce a clean straight-sets win. Total respect. The lawn just doesn't hand out fairytales on demand.

So there it is: five players, none of whom I need to win, all of whom I need to stay on their feet. €15 in, €37.57 out if grass behaves and nobody gets swept. First balls at noon, the Diallo leg at half four.
